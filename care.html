RC-I — Elderly Residential Care

A Warm Home for Your Later Years

Our elderly residential care category (RC-I) is designed for older adults who need support with daily living but wish to retain as much independence as possible. At The Glens, every resident is supported to make choices about their own life — from what they eat and when they wake, to how they spend their afternoons.

We work closely with residents and their families to create personalised care plans that evolve as needs change. Our staff are trained to recognise the signs of changing health and wellbeing, enabling timely, appropriate support at every stage.

  • Personalised daily care plans reviewed regularly with residents and families
  • Support with personal care, medication, and mobility
  • Access to GP, allied health, and specialist services
  • Dementia-aware environment and staff
RC-PH — Physical Disability Care

Specialist Care in an Accessible Environment

Our RC-PH category provides specialist residential care for those with physical disabilities. The single-storey design of The Glens is a significant advantage here — wide doorways, level flooring, and ramp access mean that physical space never becomes a barrier to independence or participation.

Weekly fees for RC-PH care are set at £837.00, reflecting the additional specialist support, adapted facilities, and enhanced staff ratios that this category requires. All fees include the mandatory £60.00 weekly top-up.

  • Fully accessible single-storey environment throughout
  • Staff trained in specialist moving and handling techniques
  • Adaptive aids and equipment provided as required
  • Liaison with physiotherapy and occupational therapy services

RC-MP — Mental Health Needs

Compassionate Support for Mental Health

Our RC-MP category provides residential care for adults with mental health needs in a calm, structured, and supportive environment. We understand that mental health conditions can be complex and fluctuating — our staff are trained to respond with patience, consistency, and compassion.

The therapeutic environment at The Glens — including its beautiful natural setting, meaningful activities, and strong community bonds — plays an important complementary role in supporting positive mental wellbeing.

  • Staff trained in mental health awareness and de-escalation
  • Structured routines that provide security and predictability
  • Close liaison with Community Mental Health Teams
  • Person-centred risk management and care planning
Staff Excellence

NISCC Registered — Mandatory Annual Training

Every member of our care team is registered with the Northern Ireland Social Care Council (NISCC) and completes a comprehensive 9-unit annual training cycle. You can be confident that the people caring for your loved one are qualified, current, and committed.

1

Dementia Awareness

Understanding dementia, person-centred approaches, and supporting residents with cognitive decline.

2

Safeguarding Adults

Recognising, reporting, and responding to abuse and neglect in a residential care context.

3

Moving & Handling

Safe moving and positioning techniques to protect both residents and staff from injury.

4

First Aid & Emergency Response

Responding confidently and correctly to medical emergencies in a residential setting.

5

Infection Prevention & Control

Maintaining a safe, hygienic environment that protects the health of all residents and staff.

6

Medication Management

Safe storage, administration, and recording of medications in line with regulatory requirements.

7

Mental Health Awareness

Supporting residents with mental health conditions with empathy, structure, and specialist knowledge.

8

Fire Safety

Prevention, evacuation procedures, and use of fire safety equipment in a residential care environment.

9

Person-Centred Care Planning

Developing, implementing, and reviewing care plans that truly reflect each resident's individual needs and wishes.

Strategic Thinking Online Training

In addition to mandatory units, all staff access the Strategic Thinking online training system and complete mandatory induction workbooks when joining The Glens. Ongoing professional development is actively supported and encouraged.
