Our History

Three Decades of Caring Excellence

The Glens Residential Home has been part of the Cushendall community for over 30 years, providing residential care that is rooted in dignity, warmth, and genuine compassion. From our earliest days, our guiding philosophy has been simple: "Homes are for living."

Located at 63 Middlepark Road, we occupy a beautifully situated single-storey bungalow that places us just moments from the marina, the beaches, and the breathtaking natural scenery of the Glens of Antrim. Our location is not incidental — it is part of the therapeutic environment we offer to every resident.

Over the decades, we have built deep relationships with the families and the wider community we serve, earning a reputation for care that is as rigorous in its standards as it is warm in its delivery.

  • 30+ years as a trusted part of the Cushendall community
  • RQIA registered and independently inspected
  • Committed to continuous improvement and excellence

Our Environment

Designed Around the People Who Live Here

Our single-storey bungalow layout is one of the most distinctive features of The Glens. Rather than a multi-floor institution, we offer a home-like layout that supports independence and ease of movement for all residents — including those with physical disabilities or mobility challenges.

We believe the physical environment of a care home makes a profound difference to quality of life. At The Glens, wide doorways, level access, and generous shared spaces are not afterthoughts — they are foundations.

  • Single-storey layout — eliminates stairs and supports maximum mobility
  • Shallow ramp access at the main entrance for wheelchair users
  • Wide doorways and corridors — generous space to prevent accidental contact
  • Personalised bedrooms — residents bring their own furniture and belongings
  • En-suite options available for additional privacy and comfort

Our Philosophy

Care That Puts People First

The Glens operates under the Statement of Purpose principle that places the individual at the centre of every decision. Our care model is guided by the seven pillars — Privacy, Dignity, Respect, Independence, Choice, Rights, and Fulfilment — which shape every interaction between staff and residents.

We are committed to enabling residents to lead lives that are as independent, active, and personally meaningful as possible. This means listening carefully, adapting our support to individual needs, and never imposing a one-size-fits-all approach.

Family involvement is genuinely valued and actively encouraged. We see the families of residents as partners in care — keeping them informed, involved, and reassured through open communication and a transparent, accessible management team.
